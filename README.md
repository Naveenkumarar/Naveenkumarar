## 🚀 About Me

I am an engineer and researcher with a strong foundation in **Embedded Systems, Robotics, Autonomous Vehicles, AI Engineering, and Full-Stack Product Development**.

My journey started close to the hardware: microcontrollers, sensors, actuators, CAN, SPI, I2C, real-time control, and vehicle systems. Over time, I moved deeper into AI, building systems around **LLMs, RAG, agents, document intelligence, semantic reasoning, and AI-powered workflows**.

Today, I focus on building practical AI systems that are not just demos, but reliable products:

* AI applications that understand documents, videos, conversations, and structured data
* RAG and multi-agent systems with traceability and grounding
* Full-stack platforms using Python, FastAPI, Next.js, React, MongoDB, Redis, and cloud infrastructure
* Robotics and autonomous systems involving ROS, control, teleoperation, perception, and human-in-the-loop interaction
* Research around semantic risk-aware control, VLM/LLM-assisted robotics, and safe autonomous navigation

I enjoy building things that combine **deep technical engineering with product thinking**.

## 🔬 Research Interests

I am especially interested in the bridge between **AI reasoning and physical robotic control**.

Current research direction:

> **Semantic Risk-Aware MPC for Human-Centered Mobile Robot Navigation**

The core idea is to use VLMs/LLMs to understand scene-level risk and convert that semantic understanding into adaptive MPC constraints, while keeping the final control layer safe, feasible, and verifiable.

Topics I care about:

* LLM/VLM-assisted robotics
* Semantic risk extraction from visual scenes
* Model Predictive Control
* Human-centered navigation
* Safe autonomous systems
* Teleoperation and haptic feedback
* Slippery surface and environment-aware motion planning

## 🧭 Engineering Philosophy

I like systems that are:

* **Useful** before they are flashy
* **Reliable** before they are complex
* **Grounded** before they are autonomous
* **Scalable** before they become painful
* **Human-centered** before they become intelligent

For me, great engineering is not only about writing code.
It is about understanding the problem deeply, choosing the right level of abstraction, and building something that survives outside the demo environment.
